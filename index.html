Projects

Biaxial Tension Rig

The device you see in the photo is a biaxial tension rig, used for characterizing materials under a biaxial load. I accomplished the following for this project: 1) I selected, routed, and assembled the electrical components for the biaxial tension rig. 2) I designed, fabricated, and assembled the clamping mechanism for the rig. 3) From scratch, I designed and implemented a control system and GUI to apply and monitor biaxial tension in thin films. The outcomes: improved accuracy and stability in acquired data, estimated cost savings of $40,000 per implementation (when compared to the existing BOSE model).

Image Processing

In order to help examine the effects of laser cutting on small, Order(mm), parts, I developed and packaged an image processing program. It is capable of taking an OD/ID part and examining two key features of the part: its concentricity, and its edge cut quality. From this application, I reccomended a new process by which process engineers can develop higher quality OD/ID parts which show significant improvement in part testing.

Path Planning

As part of a research experience, I examined higher order mathematical schemes for determining time optimal paths of sub-surface vehicles (submarines for example). I implemented these higher order schemes, using MATLAB, to determine if I could increase the resolution of the numerical solution, while maintaining or even decreasing, the processing time. The applications for these results are the following: autonomous underwater vehicles can navigate in a given water current from a point A to a point B, in the most time optimal fashion.

Carbon Nanotube Transfer Assembly

I designed, fabricated, and assembled a transfer assembly for carbon nanotubes. What exactly does this do? It is a modular piece of a larger web-handling system which takes in two ribbons (one of which is a metal film coated in carbon nanotubes, the other of which is a resin backed film) and transfers the carbon nanotubes from the metal to the resin film.

Infinity Side Table

I like woodworking (albeit I'm still not the best at it) and as such, I built a side table that has a cool affect of being "infinite". The trick is that there is a mirror in the bottom of the table and a see through mirror on the top portion.

MIT 2.007 Robot Competition

For MIT's 2.007 competition, I CAD'ed, and fabricated two RC controlled robots. The one you see is a rope climber, capable of traversing a rope at an angle of 33 degrees. The second robot is a linear actuator robot which mounts the rope climber to the rope itself.
